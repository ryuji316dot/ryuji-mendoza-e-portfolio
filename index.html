I am a Biology student at Mapúa University with a deep-seated passion for the intersection of immunology and clinical human health research. I’ve built my academic foundation around a hybrid approach—one that integrates biological fundamentals with the analytical power of informatics to gain a more comprehensive understanding of human health.

My enthusiasm for this field is rooted in my frontline experience with HIV and COVID-19 screening, where I saw firsthand how vital innovative diagnostics are to community health. These experiences have fueled my interest in how we can use informatics and molecular tools to better understand the human immune system and track emerging health threats.

I am now prepared to leverage my laboratory training and foundational knowledge of molecular systems within the broader life sciences and clinical research sector. My goal is to help bridge the gap between scientific theory and real-world outcomes, contributing to a field dedicated to protecting lives through enhanced surveillance and more efficient health systems.
